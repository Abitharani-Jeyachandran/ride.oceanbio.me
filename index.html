About Us

The Ocean Ride project is a 13 days bicycle ride around the coastal districts of Sri Lanka, covering over 1300+km to promote marine conservation, create awareness among the local population about the importance of taking care of the ocean, responsible utilization of ocean resources, and creating inspiration to impact the future generation.

Who are we

We are young and passionate advocates for marine conservation, part of the Ocean Biome social enterprise, voluntarily taking on the challenge of riding over 1300km around the coastal districts of Sri Lanka on bicycles to spread awareness and be an inspiration.


What is our mission

To inspire the young generation to proactively work towards protecting the ocean and its resources around Sri Lanka by providing motivation, raising awareness and conducting education.

What are we going to do

Promote marine conservation

Sri Lanka was ranked among the top five marine plastic polluters of the world by earthday.org in 2019 with a mismanaged plastic waste of 1.59 million metric tons per year. There is an urgent need to raise awareness among society, institutions, and the government to address marine plastic pollution problems. This ride itself is to put attention on this dire issue of ocean pollution and depletion of marine resources and to raise awareness on sustainable and responsible consumption of the treasure trove of marine resources.

Promote physical health

Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) such as heart disease, stroke, cancer, diabetes, and chronic lung disease cause over 80% of deaths in Sri Lanka. Over 1/4th of the population is overweight. We would like to address these issues as well by promoting the importance of aerobic physical activities such as cycling.

Promote zero emission green transportation

In 2020, CO2 emissions from transport for Sri Lanka were 9 metric tons. Many public and private transportation methods still use primarily fossil fuels which adds to the climate change crisis. Through this long-distance bicycle ride, we aim to motivate our fellow citizens to choose clean transportation methods and reduce their carbon footprint.

Promote the drive towards Blue Economy

With over 517,000 sq.km of exclusive economic zone on the ocean belonging to Sri Lanka which is around 8 times our land mass, why should our country be classed as lower-middle-income and our primary contributor to GDP be the service sector? We want to put attention to the importance of developing a sustainable blue economy and to improve the livelihood of over 33% of total population who depend on ocean resources residing in coastal areas.
